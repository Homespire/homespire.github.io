One Borrower. Three Ways to Yes.

The Situation

Maria runs a successful catering business — six years in, $180,000 flowing through her accounts every year. But after legitimate deductions, her tax return shows $52,000. She's been denied twice by her bank. She has great credit, money saved, and she's ready to buy a $450,000 home.

Maria doesn't have one option.
She has three.

Bank Statement Loan
24 months of deposits average $15,000/mo. Her real income — not her tax return — gets her approved.
P&L Only
Her CPA provides a signed Profit & Loss statement. We use that as income documentation instead.
Asset Qualifier
If Maria has $600K+ in liquid assets, she qualifies with zero income documentation required.

Scenario is illustrative. Actual eligibility subject to individual qualification review.
